Q1: What is air pollution?
A1: Air pollution is the presence of harmful substances in the atmosphere, such as gases, particulates, and biological molecules, which can harm human health and the environment.

Q2: Which season has the highest air pollution?
A2: Winter season usually has the highest pollution because of temperature inversion, low wind speeds, and increased burning activities.

Q3: Which season has the lowest air pollution?
A3: The rainy or monsoon season has the lowest air pollution, as rain helps wash away dust and pollutants from the air.

Q4: What are the main causes of air pollution in Delhi?
A4: Major causes include vehicle emissions, industrial pollution, construction dust, crop residue burning, and weather conditions that trap pollutants.

Q5: How does rain help reduce air pollution?
A5: Rain removes pollutants by capturing them in raindrops and carrying them down to the ground, a process called wet deposition.

Q13: What are the health effects of air pollution?
A13: Air pollution can cause respiratory diseases, heart problems, asthma, and reduced lung function.

Q14: How can we reduce air pollution?
A14: Use public transport, reduce burning of waste, plant trees, use clean energy sources, and follow emission standards.

Q15: What is PM2.5 and PM10?
A15: PM2.5 and PM10 are particulate matter with diameters less than 2.5 and 10 micrometers respectively. They can enter lungs and cause health problems.
